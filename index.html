The read

SOFWERX has moved $460 million through 1,629 purchase orders and B2B agreements since 2015. The 46% prototype-to-Phase-3 transition rate sits well above the DoD baseline, and the 30-day commercial agreement cycle is the kind of throughput that the rest of the acquisition community is still trying to reproduce. Matt Creamer, Eduba's CRO, walked the SOFWERX floor at eMerge Americas because the number above the numbers is the one that matters: the 54% of prototypes that do not transition. That is where a methodology conversation starts.

Eduba's operating thesis is a 60/30/10 split. About 60% of the work in a document-heavy synthesis pipeline is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. About 10% is a genuine AI problem. Teams that skip this distinction put LLMs where Postgres would do the job faster, cheaper, and with better audit guarantees. Applied to SOFWERX, the RFI triage and transition-tracking workflows are primarily a structured-data and rules problem, with a narrow AI layer on top for synthesis. The design question is which piece of each workflow belongs on which layer. That is the conversation Matt walked up with.

Closest precedent in Eduba's practice

The closest precedent inside Eduba's practice is VigilOre, a multi-agent compliance platform for DRC mining operations. Dense document synthesis, regulated environment, long hours of manual review. Eduba's delivery collapsed 160+ hours of compliance work to under 5 minutes per cycle. The engagement is a scoped sprint followed by a long-term methodology-transfer relationship. The shape of the work maps onto SOFWERX's assessment-event triage and transition-pipeline tracking almost directly, allowing for the obvious differences in domain and governance.

The methodology underneath

The methodology underneath the delivery is the Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM TiiS and open-sourced on MIT license. ICM organizes agent context as a layered filesystem so that the behavior of an agentic workflow is inspectable the same way a well-run SOP is inspectable. For a nonprofit operating under PIA authority and reporting up to USSOCOM, that auditability is load-bearing, not a marketing feature. Repo: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.

For governance-heavy reviews, Eduba also maintains the Ethics Engine, a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. Paper: arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742. Repo: github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine.
